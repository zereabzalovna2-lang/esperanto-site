Esperanto is an international auxiliary language created in 1887 by Ludwig Lazarus Zamenhof, a Polish-Jewish ophthalmologist. His goal was to design a simple, politically neutral language that could foster peace and international understanding.

‚ú® Why Esperanto?

  • It is easy to learn thanks to regular grammar and phonetic spelling.
  • It helps build bridges between cultures and people worldwide.
  • There are active Esperanto communities in more than 120 countries.
  • It is used in literature, music, conferences, and even some schools.
